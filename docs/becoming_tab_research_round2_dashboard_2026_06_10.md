# Becoming tab round 2: the aesthetic dashboard brief
2026-06-10 · senior product designer, data-rich consumer apps · round 2
**The founder's correction (verbatim):** "design-wise it needs to be aesthetic,
modern, simple, appealing, useful, a lot of useful information in one snapshot
like dashboard (but not the boring one)." Round 1 optimized for editorial
restraint (5 zones, gets quieter, 3 numbers). Round 2 solves **density with
beauty**: a real dashboard's worth of data in one glance, executed 2026-premium.
This mirrors the standing correction on record (feedback_visual_richness_over_
restraint): when research pulls toward austerity, layer discipline ON TOP of
the brand's richness, never subtract to a sparse page.

## §1 Density mechanics extracted from the masters

How the best data apps pack 6–10 data points into a viewport without reading
as a spreadsheet. Mechanical, not vibes:

**Copilot Money** (ADA finalist 2024, ex-Apple team)
- **The triplet atom.** Every metric renders as the same 3-part anatomy:
  small muted label → large numeral → tiny context (delta or sparkline).
  Density is survivable because the eye learns ONE shape and reads it 8 times.
- **Color is category, never mood.** Each spending category owns one muted
  hue used at tiny sizes (icon chip, bar segment). The numerals stay neutral.
  Result: 9 colors on screen, zero noise, because color never competes with type.
- **Numbers do the design work.** Hero numerals at ~34pt, secondary at 17pt,
  context at 13pt — a strict 3-step numeric scale. No numeral ever appears at
  an in-between size. The scale ladder IS the hierarchy; cards barely exist.
- **Depth-on-tap is total.** The surface shows only the headline read of each
  metric; every tile expands to a full-screen chart. The dashboard is a table
  of contents, not the book.

**Gentler Streak** (ADA winner 2024, the anti-shame benchmark)
- **One drawn artifact encodes 5 dimensions.** The "activity path" wave holds
  training load, trend, today's position, the safe zone, and rest state in a
  single organic shape with no axes — one bespoke visualization absorbing what
  would otherwise be 4 tiles.
- **Data translated into words.** The status is a sentence ("go gentler")
  ABOVE the visualization; the chart is the receipt, language is the verdict.
  Soft palette + paper texture make clinical data feel homey — warmth is a
  rendering decision, not a content decision.

**Apple Fitness Summary** — consistent tile anatomy at high density (label →
bold numeral+unit → micro-chart thumbnail) under one rings hero; the grid
reads fast because every tile answers in the same grammar. Units set smaller
and lighter than numbers — tiny move, huge de-spreadsheeting effect.

**Oura Today (Oct 2025 redesign)** — "one big thing" hero + a timeline of
small chips; the score wall was explicitly cut, everything else one tap deep
(Vitals). Color signals body-state, never decoration. Discoveries =
correlational sentences about HER habits: narrative-over-chart, the 2026
direction (matches round-1 app-expert brief §3).

**Flo Today** — insight cards carry the key fact + a normal/not indicator
only; full explanations live on separate screens (their tested pattern), and
illustration softens raw numbers — the warmth layer this cohort expects.

**Cal AI** — rings pair big bold numerals INSIDE the arc so the number reads
without decoding it; horizontal stat carousel = density by swipe, not by
shrinking; date strip gives temporal context in 24pt of height.

**Amie** (Golden Kitty, design) — components-first: a tiny set of atoms reused
everywhere makes density feel designed; joy via micro-interaction, not chrome.

**How We Feel** (ADA finalist 2024) — a 100-cell emotion matrix that never
reads as a spreadsheet because color+shape ENCODE the axes; even a literal
grid is beautiful when the encoding is felt, not labeled.

**The seven transferable mechanics:**
1. One hero visual per viewport; every other metric is number-first, chart-as-thumbnail.
2. One repeated atom anatomy (label → numeral → context) so density scans as rhythm.
3. Strict numeric type ladder (3 sizes, no in-betweens); units/captions demoted.
4. Color = meaning only; data hues ≤2 on the whole surface; numerals stay ink-colored.
5. Sparklines naked (no axes/gridlines), thumbnail-sized, always beside a numeral.
6. Verdicts in language above the data; the chart is the receipt.
7. Surface = table of contents; every module opens a depth sheet.

## §2 The screenshot-worthy dashboard language, 2026

What makes young women screenshot a data screen (Pinterest "wellness dashboard"
boards, Fitness-Wrapped chart-toppers, her75's share loop):

- **It reads as an identity artifact, not a report.** her75's day-one card
  works because it has a date range, a horizon, and HER list — a planner page,
  not a stat sheet. The screenshot says "I am a person who," never "here are
  my numbers."
- **Oversized expressive type is the 2026 carrier.** The bento wave matured:
  bento is now understood as information architecture (tile size = data
  priority), while the aesthetic moved to expressive/editorial typography —
  oversized numerals AS the decoration. A 44pt serif "day 23" outperforms any
  illustration.
- **Wrapped-grammar:** one hero number + 3 supporting facts + a date + soft
  background = the shareable unit (Spotify Wrapped → Fitness Wrapped apps).
  Never more than 4 facts on a share card.
- **Warm-neutral canvases beat dark mode for this cohort.** Mercury proved
  dark-cinematic = luxury for fintech bros; the "that girl" register is cream,
  beige, blush — JeniFit's bgPrimary is already the correct canvas.
- **No visible app chrome in the screenshot zone.** Status bars, tab bars,
  buttons kill the post. The top ~70% of the viewport (folio + spread) must
  compose as a clean portrait crop.
- **Her own photos are the richness.** Pinterest dashboards that travel mix
  data with polaroid-style personal photos. JeniFit's plate filmstrip is the
  moat no competitor screenshot has.

**The screenshot test for any Becoming layout:** crop the top 70% — does it
look like a page from her planner (post it) or a settings screen (don't)?

## §3 The JeniFit synthesis: editorial masthead × disciplined density

**Resolving round 1 explicitly.** The round-1 brief was right about REGISTERS
(chrome ladder, language-not-arrows, one chromed artifact, serif folio) and
wrong about BUDGET (48pt air bands, one number per zone, page that fades out).
The founder's correction keeps the ladder and triples the data on it. What
changes:

| Round 1 | Round 2 |
|---|---|
| Folio = ~40% of viewport, vast air | Folio compressed to ~22%, masthead-style |
| One metric per zone, 3 visible numbers | 8–10 data points above the fold |
| zero 2-up grids ever | ONE editorial stat-pair row allowed (hairline column rule, no tile borders) |
| page fades to footnotes | page ends on the insight sentence + photo strip (richness, not silence) |
| ledger rows 44pt, airy | ledger rows 40pt, sparkline-annotated |

**The print analogy shifts.** Round 1 designed a feature spread (one image,
vast margins). Round 2 designs the magazine's **"by the numbers" front-of-book
page**: dense, gridded, beautiful — because the grid is disciplined and the
typography does the aesthetics. Vogue runs dense index pages; they never read
as Excel.

**Who does serif-led data design well:**
- **Mercury** — luxury read via a single custom face + one accent + generous
  leading; density survives because chrome is near-zero and type carries
  hierarchy. Steal: hierarchy-by-type-not-boxes.
- **Bloomberg Businessweek / FT data pages** — serif headlines over dense
  sans-set figures, hairline rules, annotations set like captions. Steal: the
  two-typeface contract (serif speaks, sans counts).
- **her75** — serif numerals in LISTS (sticky-note checklist, numbered day-one
  facts), never in tables. Steal: serif numerals are display objects.

**The typographic contract (load-bearing, from type research):** oldstyle/
display serif figures are beautiful in running text and heroes but measurably
hard to scan in tables. So: **Fraunces numerals ONLY at display sizes
(≥20pt, max 3 instances per viewport); DM Sans tabular figures for every
dense row and micro-label.** This single rule is what lets JeniFit be dense
AND editorial at once — the serif stays a luxury voice, the sans does the
bookkeeping.

**Where the coquette warmth lives at density** (the brand, per
feedback_visual_richness_over_restraint): the cream canvas + rose hairlines +
accent micro-bars + her plate photos + one heart terminal punctuation + the
scrapbook-chromed artifact card. NOT stickers (scatter-milestone rule holds:
Becoming is a daily surface, not an earned moment) and NOT more borders.
Richness = photo + color-in-data + serif voice, on a disciplined grid.

## §4 Component vocabulary (dashboard atoms + register rules)

| # | Atom | Anatomy | Register rules | Max/viewport |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Folio masthead** | serif day-count ("day twenty-three") 34–42pt + "of her 84" + date range + identity line | top of page only; no card; reads `plan.totalDays`, never "75" | exactly 1 |
| 2 | **Serif hero stat** | Fraunces Light numeral ≥20pt + DM Sans 13pt lowercase caption | display slots only (folio, artifact card, stat pair); NEVER inside ledger rows | 3 |
| 3 | **Ledger row** | DM Sans 15pt label · right-aligned DM Sans SemiBold tabular numeral · optional atom-4/5 between · hairline divider | 40pt height, no fill, no border; every row taps to a depth sheet | 3–5 rows |
| 4 | **Micro sparkline** | 1.5pt cocoa line, ~48×16pt, no axes/dots/fill | only beside a numeral (never standalone); EMA or 7-day shape only | 2 |
| 5 | **Macro micro-bars** | the shipped HomeFoodCard 3-bar (protein/carb/fat, 3pt capsules, accent + tints) | inside its ledger row or the stat pair; never with numeric % labels on the surface | 1 cluster |
| 6 | **Week dot-row** | 7 dots, 6pt: filled cocoa = done, `divider`-stroke open = not-yet; today ringed | gain-framed (never red, never X); pairs with a "5 of 7" count | 1 |
| 7 | **Stat pair** | 2-up, separated by a 1pt hairline COLUMN rule (magazine columns), each cell = atom 2 or 3 anatomy | the only 2-up allowed; no tile backgrounds/borders — the hairline is the entire chrome | 1 row |
| 8 | **Photo-thumb strip** | 4-up of her plate photos, 8pt corners, 56pt tall | her photos only; collapses entirely when empty (never placeholder) | 1 |
| 9 | **Insight line** | one sentence, DM Sans 14pt, ONE italic-Fraunces punch word, optional ♥ | data-provenance gated (app-expert §3 ranking); rotates weekly; never a card | exactly 1 |
| 10 | **Delta-in-language chip** | lowercase phrase ("down, gently" / "holding steady"), textSecondary | replaces ALL arrows/±%; sage `stateGood` text allowed for goal-hit only | 2 |
| 11 | **Artifact card** | full scrapbook chrome (24pt, 1.5pt accent border, rose shadow) wrapping atoms 2+4+10 | the ONLY bordered card; weight trend by default, swaps to adherence when weight stale/hidden | exactly 1 |

**Ring policy:** 0 rings on Becoming. Rings are Cal AI's signature and Home's
TodayHealthStrip future; on this tab dots (binary days) + bars (composition) +
sparklines (trend) cover every shape of data. One ring max EVER, and only if
it replaces the dot-row, never alongside it.

**Color-in-data budget:** accent rose (bars, today-dot ring) + cocoa (lines,
filled dots) + sage (goal-hit language only). Numerals are always
textPrimary/textSecondary — color never sits on a number.

## §5 The one-snapshot viewport (iPhone 15, ~393×852pt)

Core read with NO scroll: program day · week adherence · steps today/week ·
plate pattern · trend direction in language · one insight.

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                    ☰     │ thin menu mark
│  day twenty-three          ← Fraunces 36, "twenty-three" italic
│  of her 84 · apr 2 → jun 25              │ DM Sans 13 textSecondary
│  becoming steady.                        │ identity line, italic punch
│                                          │ (~12pt air)
│  this week              5 of 7   ●●●●●○◐ │ eyebrow + serif count 20pt
│  ──────────────────────────────────────  │   + week dot-row (atom 6)
│ ┌──────────────────────────────────────┐ │
│ │ trend                            👁  │ │ THE artifact card (atom 11)
│ │ 142.6        down, gently            │ │ Fraunces Light 34 + language
│ │      ·──~──‿──﹏──‿_                  │ │ naked EMA sparkline, 2
│ │  apr 2ᵎ              ᵎyou, today     │ │ print-caption annotations
│ └──────────────────────────────────────┘ │
│                                          │
│  steps               │  plates           │ STAT PAIR (atom 7),
│  6,240 today         │  ▂▆▃ balanced     │ hairline column rule;
│  41,200 this week ⌁  │  protein led, 4   │ tabular numerals + micro-
│                      │  of 5 days        │ bars + language chip
│  ──────────────────────────────────────  │
│  her week in plates                      │ eyebrow
│  [🍳][🥗][🍜][🍓]                          │ photo strip (atom 8)
│                                          │
│  most of your movement lands             │ insight line (atom 9),
│  after 6pm. *evening* girl. ♥            │ italic punch, provenance-
│                                          │ gated rotation
├──────────────────────────────────────────┤
│        today        ·     becoming       │ tab bar
└──────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

Ten data points (day, total, horizon, adherence count, 7 day-states, trend
numeral, trend direction, steps today, steps week, plate composition + cadence)
plus one insight — and the top 70% crops to a clean planner-page screenshot.

**Depth map (where scroll/sheets carry the rest):**

| Tap | Opens |
|---|---|
| folio | day-card share sheet (9:16 artifact, her75 day-one DNA, round-1 §3b) |
| this-week row / dots | checklist depth: per-day breakdown, banked-tiles total, barrier insight |
| artifact card | full trend sheet: raw dots, ACSM pace ghost-line, range picker, weigh-in log |
| steps cell | steps depth: weekly bars, 7,500 anchor context, movement-timing fact |
| plates cell | food depth: macro week view, eating-window reality, protein floor (GLP-1 copy) |
| photo strip | meal scrapbook (full filmstrip → future Pinterest-coded surface) |
| insight line | "why this" footnote sheet: the data + citation behind the sentence |
| below the fold (scroll) | weekly recap card (blooms Sundays), plank mastery curve, lesson shelf, cohort care-lines |

The recap takeover, milestone moments, and sticker wax-seal question all stand
as specified in round 1 §5 — this round changes the daily surface only.

## §6 Anti-boring rules: the 10 tells of 2014-fitness-app, and the replacement move

| # | The 2014 tell | The replacement move |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Equal-weight tile grid, every module in the same box | Mixed registers on one grid: masthead → dots → one chromed card → hairline pair → photos. Size & chrome = priority (bento-as-IA, not bento-as-style) |
| 2 | Charts with axes, gridlines, legends | Naked sparkline + 2 print-caption annotations ("apr 2", "you, today"). The depth sheet holds the full chart |
| 3 | Red/green delta arrows and ± percentages | Direction in language ("down, gently", "holding steady"); sage text only for goal-hit |
| 4 | A ring for everything | 0 rings; dots for binary days, micro-bars for composition, sparkline for trend |
| 5 | ALL-CAPS BOLD LABELS over every number | 12pt lowercase eyebrow; the numeral outweighs its label 3:1 in size |
| 6 | White cards on grey, borders on everything | Cream canvas, hairline rules, exactly ONE bordered artifact. Boxes are earned |
| 7 | Flame/trophy/dumbbell icon set | No metric icons. Type, dots, and her photos carry identity; the only glyphs are the menu mark and eye toggle |
| 8 | Today's weight as the hero number | Program day is the masthead; trend (EMA) is the artifact; today's scale number never headlines (trend-as-hero lock) |
| 9 | Progress bars labeled "71%" | Serif counts in words and fractions: "5 of 7", "day twenty-three". Percentages live in depth sheets if anywhere |
| 10 | Stock photos / illustration filler in empty slots | Her own plate photos or full collapse. An empty module disappears; it never apologizes with a placeholder |

Plus the two meta-rules that keep density premium: **numerals on the 3-step
ladder only** (34/20/15 — an in-between size is the first smell of spreadsheet),
and **every pixel of color on the surface means something** (category, state,
or brand-punch — never fill).
