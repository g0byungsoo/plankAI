# Becoming calorie integration — WL iOS design expert + WL program expert

**Date:** 2026-06-06 (founder feedback round 10)
**Trigger:** Founder questioned the 40pt identity hero ("becoming *powerful*.") and asked: shouldn't a WL app emphasize calorie spent + calorie gained more?

2 expert briefs in parallel: **WL iOS design expert** (Cal AI / MFP / MacroFactor / Lose It / WW veteran) + **WL program expert + RD** (Gen-Z women WL behavioral specialist).

---

## Unanimous verdict

### KILL the 40pt identity hero
- **WL design**: "Brand poetry, not tool value. WL cohorts who open a Becoming tab are not asking 'who am I?' — they're asking 'did I move the needle today?' Cal AI doesn't have a hero. MFP killed theirs in 2023. MacroFactor never had one. WW MyDay opens on the SmartPoints budget. The pattern is unanimous: WL tab opens on the answer to today's question."
- **WL program**: "Founder's instinct ('gives almost no value') is correct about the *current execution*. A 40pt 'becoming powerful' hero is a splash screen — works once, then becomes wallpaper."

### Identity SURVIVES — as a CAPTION on data, not a standalone card
- **Program expert** (the resolution): "Identity attached to evidence is adherence-driving. Identity floating alone is decoration."
- Example caption pattern: "your trend says -0.6 lb/wk — *becoming powerful* takes consistency, and you're showing it."
- Q140 punch word moves to subtext on the weight trend card OR above the streak strip as one quiet line.

### The signature card both experts pushed for
**"Today's balance" / "trend deficit"** — but with critical framing nuance:

| Expert | Daily vs Weekly | Framing |
|---|---|---|
| **WL design** | Daily card "today's balance" with "−570 kcal deficit" headline + horizontal bar (gained left, spent right) | Direct numeric — "deficit" in subhead, "today" in headline |
| **WL program** | **Weekly rolling** ("your body is running a ~340 kcal deficit this week") — daily creates restriction-binge cycling | **Trend-derived from math, not prescribed AT her**. "Direct calorie display = MFP. Color zones = Noom. Trend-derived deficit = the JeniFit wedge." |

→ **Resolution**: Build BOTH — daily-balance card (front-of-house) + weekly-trend card (deeper). But the weekly one is the moat per program expert.

### Calorie SPENT vs GAINED — the critical asymmetry
**Program expert insight (worth ignoring at JeniFit's peril):**
> "calories IN is 80% of WL variance, calories OUT from exercise is <15%. Showing 'calories burned this workout' trains the exact wrong mental model — the 'I earned this cookie' loop that drove MFP's churn problem for a decade."

→ **Verdict**: HIDE raw exercise calories. Show intake + trend deficit. Calories spent breakdown OK as a quieter secondary card but never as a "you earned X" framing.

### Voice + chrome
- **pageIvory fill** for calorie cards, NOT pink. "Pink fills on a deficit card would feel like the app is trying to apologize for the math." (Design expert)
- italic-Fraunces lands on subhero punch words ("*toward* goal", "*ahead* of plan", "*honest* picture") — NEVER on numbers
- Hearts: ONE per Becoming tab MAX, only on the signature card when she hits deficit. Scarcity is the signal.

## The 5 behavioral metrics that ACTUALLY drive WL outcomes (program expert)

1. **Self-monitoring consistency** (logging days/week — Hollis 2008 DPP: 5+/week = 2× weight loss)
2. **Self-weigh frequency** (Helander 2014: gaps >1 week predict regain within 90 days)
3. **Sleep duration + regularity** (Tasali 2022: +1.2hr sleep = -270 kcal/day spontaneous intake)
4. **Identity congruence with future self** (Oyserman 2015: vivid 6-month-self → 40% longer adherence)
5. **Movement consistency, not intensity** (showing up matters; calories burned per session doesn't)

**Notice what's NOT on this list:** ring closure, BMI band, calories burned per workout, streaks-as-streaks. Those are proxies, not levers.

7. **Logging consistency** ("you logged 5 of 7 days" — self-monitoring drives self-monitoring per Burke 2011)
