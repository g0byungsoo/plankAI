# Home + Becoming — luxury-warm visual direction (2026-06-06)

## Executive visual direction (1 paragraph)

JeniFit's Home + Becoming should run on a single composition principle borrowed from Aesop, Hermès, and Cereal — **air is the primary design element, the coquette sticker is the signature mark, never the surface noise**. Today the surfaces are good but compete: cocoa note + food card + JeniMethod + steps/breath + workout all read "card, card, card, card, card" on a cream background. The fix is to treat Home as a **single editorial spread** (one hero with intentional whitespace, then a quietly-numbered rhythm of smaller blocks) and Becoming as a **5-chapter editorial publication** (each chapter opens with a full-width title spread before content, like flipping a Cereal page) rather than 5 stacked dashboard sections. The coquette stickers stay — but they get tightened to one signature sticker per surface, sized 28–44pt, rotated 6–12°, opacity 100%, always at margins, never inside data. The italic-Fraunces punch word + lowercase casual voice already does the Gen-Z warmth work; the visual layer's job is to **stop competing with it**. Luxury-warm in 2026 is not "minimal vs. coquette" — it's coquette warmth on a Chanel-disciplined armature. Pick the cleaner option even when it feels emptier; the warmth comes from the one element you kept, not the ones you added.

## 1. The luxury-minimal axis — Chanel / Hermès / Aesop

**What the references actually do in 2026:**

- **Chanel.com** — custom sans (AB Chanel Corpo) in all-caps with extra letterspacing; cream-to-white backgrounds; products photographed against neutral planes with extreme whitespace around them. Hierarchy is set by **scale + air**, not by weight contrast or borders. The page reads as a sequence of single hero images per scroll viewport, each with one title and a thin caption ([Mojomox: What font does Chanel use](https://fonts.mojomox.com/blogs/brand-fonts/chanel-font)).
- **Hermès.com (Jan 2026 "Venture Beyond")** — illustrated underwater banner + 12 hand-drawn icon-illustrations for each product category, lithographic style, warm gradient glow. The store became "an immersive editorial communication" rather than a catalog. One illustrated hero per category section, near-zero chrome around it ([Domus on Hermès new website](https://www.domusweb.it/en/news/2026/01/07/herms-new-website.html), [It's Nice That on Linda Merad's Hermès illustrations](https://www.itsnicethat.com/articles/linda-merad-hermes-illustration-project-090426)).
- **Aesop.com** — apothecary restraint. Editorial discipline, long-form product storytelling, near-zero visual noise. The Fabulist editorial property sits inside the digital ecosystem as a brand pillar, not a sidebar ([Aesop case study, Work & Co](https://work.co/clients/aesop/)). 2026 ecommerce design has bifurcated into luxury-restraint (Aesop, Bottega, Patagonia) vs. challenger maximalism (Glossier, Poppi, Oatly) — both are legitimate; JeniFit is not in either pure camp.

**What JeniFit can adopt:**

- **One hero per viewport.** Today Home crams 5 cards into the first scroll. Adopt Chanel rule: the cocoa "today, jen ♥" note + one hero action (food OR lesson, whichever has agency right now) gets the entire above-fold. Everything else lives below an intentional 56pt cream gap.
- **Thin marks instead of borders.** The 1.5pt cocoa scrapbook border is correct as a signature, but only one or two cards per screen should wear it. The rest should sit on cream with no border at all — separated only by a 0.5pt cocoa hairline rule or 24pt of air. Aesop and Cereal both separate sections with whitespace + hairline, not contained cards.
- **Numbered/lettered chapter labels.** Hermès marks sections with a letter or roman numeral in tiny all-caps DM Sans (10pt, letterspacing +80) before the chapter title. JeniFit Becoming should do this — `I. your week`, `II. what you ate`, etc. — as the editorial cue.

**ASCII — Home today vs. Home Aesop-disciplined:**

├─────────────────────────┤         │   (single ring,         │

├─────────────────────────┤         │    no border)           │

## 2. The Gen-Z warmth axis — Glossier / Mejuri / Skims

**What the references actually do:**

- **Glossier** — millennial pink + warm cream (#FCE6E9, #FDD7C4), rounded typefaces, soft photography on cream surfaces. The 2026 evolution is "welcoming minimalism" — slightly rounded corners, warmer background tones, typography with personality, replacing the sterile-flat-white era ([Glossier color palette, color-hex.com](https://www.color-hex.com/color-palette/1057205), [Skyrye Design on 2026 minimalism](https://skyryedesign.com/design/minimalism-in-design-complete-expert-guide/)).
- **Mejuri** — conversational lowercase headlines ("jewelry you can live in", "you deserve the damn diamond"). Product hero is a single styled lifestyle shot, warm natural tones, body-on-skin photography. Cards are large, generous, never gridded into a busy catalog. Bright + calming store ambiance, white + gold ([Mejuri.com](https://mejuri.com/)).
- **Skims app** — scalable design system blending editorial storytelling with ecommerce. Bento layout with rounded rectangular cards, generous padding, distinct background colors instead of borders ([ArcTouch Skims case study](https://arctouch.com/portfolio/skims), [Layout Scene card UI guide](https://www.layoutscene.com/card-ui-design-patterns-guide-2026/)).

**What JeniFit can adopt:**

- **Cream + one warm photo per surface.** Mejuri's warmth comes from one editorial photograph per scroll, not from chrome or texture. JeniFit's equivalent: the cocoa note is the editorial photograph stand-in (the only "hot" element above fold), the data below stays muted.
- **Conversational lowercase as the warmth signature** — already locked. Keep going harder on this. "your week ♥" is right. "what you ate" is right. Anything that drifts toward "weekly summary" or "nutrition overview" breaks the register.
- **Bento with implicit grouping, not bordered cards.** Steps + breath strip should sit on cream with shared padding, separated from food hero by 32pt of air, not by chrome. This is the Skims 2026 pattern.

## 3. The blend point — where Chanel meets Glossier

This is the load-bearing question. The blend rule, derived from the references:

**Composition rule: 80% restraint, 20% warmth — and the 20% must be ONE element.**

Concretely:
- **Whitespace dominance**: every surface should pass the squint test — if you blur your eyes, you should see mostly cream with one warm focal point and one signature mark. If you see two equally-hot zones, cut one.
- **Signature mark vs. surface noise**: the coquette sticker is the JeniFit equivalent of the Chanel CC monogram or the Hermès anchor-chain motif. **One signature per surface.** Hermès uses one illustration per category page, not twelve. Apply the same rule.
- **One italic-Fraunces punch per chapter**. Currently Becoming has italic Fraunces in 5 chapter titles + multiple inline. Cap it at the title + one mid-chapter pull-phrase, max. Hermès and Loewe both use their custom typefaces sparingly — the punch is in the contrast, not the volume.
- **Cocoa pill is the brand's CC monogram.** Treat it that way: it appears once above-fold (the CTA), once in the navigation, never inside a card as decoration. The note treatment ("today, jen ♥") is the exception because it's the editorial-voice device.
- **Photography temperature**: Mejuri/Glossier shoot in warm afternoon light; Chanel/Aesop shoot in cool daylight. JeniFit's coquette stickers do the temperature work, so photography/illustration should be **cool-neutral cream backgrounds**, not warm peach. Two warm sources compete; the sticker should win.

**Rule of thumb for the founder:** when deciding between two layouts, **always pick the one with fewer hot elements above the fold** — the warmth signature gets louder the less it competes with.

## 4. Sticker discipline — bow, flower, hearts, sparkle, cherries

This is the highest-risk axis. The same sticker pack that reads coquette-warm at 1 per surface reads MySpace 2007 at 4 per surface.

**The discipline (derived from how Hermès, Loewe, and Glossier handle signature marks):**

| dimension | rule | rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **count per surface** | 1 hero sticker + max 1 peripheral | Hermès uses 1 illustration per category. Anything more dilutes the signature. |
| **size** | hero 36–48pt, peripheral 20–28pt | Smaller than the headline. Larger than the body. Never the focal point. |
| **placement** | corners + margins only, NEVER over data | The Glossier 2026 "welcoming minimalism" rule: warmth at the edges, data in the center stays calm. |
| **rotation** | 6–14° from horizontal | Less than 6° looks like a bug. More than 15° looks like deliberate chaos. The Mejuri/Loewe sweet spot is ~8–10°. |
| **opacity** | 100% on cream; never tint stickers | The sticker IS the warmth. Lowering opacity makes it look broken. If a sticker is "too loud" at 100%, the answer is to make it smaller or remove it, never to fade it. |
| **density per scroll** | 1 sticker per viewport-height max | Hermès' 12 illustrations are 1 per category page, not 12 per page. Apply the same. |
| **categorical anchoring** | each surface owns a sticker family | Bow = identity/notes. Flower3D = becoming/data. Hearts = celebration/streak. Sparkle = lessons/method. Cherries = food. This builds visual associations like Glossier's Cloud Paint colors do — semantic, not decorative. |
| **animation** | tap-only spring, no autoplay | A sticker that wiggles on every appear becomes a Webkinz site. Animation should reward intent. |

**ASCII — three placements, ranked:**

```
RIGHT                       WRONG                        WRONG-2
┌───────────────┐           ┌───────────────┐            ┌───────────────┐
│            ◠bow│           │   ◠bow        │            │ ◠bow      ❀   │
│ today, jen ♥  │           │ ◠ ❀  today    │            │ today, jen ♥  │
│               │           │ ❀ ◠ jen ♥     │            │  ❀ ✦   ♥      │
│  HERO         │           │  ✦ ◠  ❀       │            │ ✦  HERO   ✦   │
│               │           │  HERO with    │            │   ◠           │
│           ❀   │           │  stickers OVER│            │ ❀  ❀  ◠  ✦    │
└───────────────┘           └───────────────┘            └───────────────┘
1 hero + 1 margin           confetti over data           every sticker, no air
```

## 5. Typography hierarchy — Fraunces + DM Sans

**Current state**: Fraunces display + DM Sans body, italic-Fraunces punch words, lowercase casual. The voice is correct. The hierarchy is muddled.

**What luxury-Gen-Z does in 2026:**
- Cereal Magazine uses **6 type styles total** for the entire magazine, 8.5pt body serif, 6pt sans for credits, hierarchy from negative space not size ([Studio Faculty on Cereal Magazine redesign](https://www.studiofaculty.com/work/cereal-magazine)).
- Glossier and Skims drive identity through type-as-hero — oversized headlines, confident scale, no graphic treatment ([Skyrye Design](https://skyryedesign.com/design/minimalism-in-design-complete-expert-guide/)).
- 2026 trend across luxury + Gen-Z is **serif revival** with personality (Fraunces sits perfectly here) and **typewriter mono** for labels/captions (Le Labo's signature — GT America + Courier-style mono) ([Le Labo typography, The Brand Identity](https://the-brandidentity.com/project/damien-berhelot-nada-bennani-combine-rebrand-french-menswear-boutique-le-labo)).

**Specific JeniFit recommendations:**

| token | size | weight | line-height | letterspacing | usage |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `hero` | 36pt Fraunces | 400 | 1.05 | -1.5% | one per surface — chapter open, hero card title |
| `heroAccent` | 36pt Fraunces Italic | 400 | 1.05 | -1.5% | ONE punch word inside hero, not the whole line |
| `section` | 22pt Fraunces | 500 | 1.15 | -0.5% | Becoming chapter title (`your week`) |
| `eyebrow` | 10pt DM Sans | 500 | 1.0 | +120ms (12%) all-caps | roman numeral / category label above section |
| `body` | 15pt DM Sans | 400 | 1.4 | 0 | body copy |
| `caption` | 12pt DM Sans | 400 | 1.3 | +20ms | timestamps, data labels, captions |
| `numeric` | 28–88pt Fraunces tabular | 500 | 1.0 | -2% | weight, time, calories — Fraunces NOT DM Sans for big numbers (this is the Tiffany/Mejuri move; numbers feel jeweled) |

**Copy this from Mejuri:** lowercase headlines run all the way down. Even your eyebrow labels.

**Avoid this from Gen-Z trends:** kinetic type animation, oversized mixed-script word art, neon contrast type, gothic decoratives. These cluster under "Gen-Z maximalism" which JeniFit's locked direction explicitly rejects ([Memorable Design on minimalist vs maximalist 2026](https://memorable.design/minimalist-brand-identity/)).

## 6. Color discipline

**Current palette:**
- Cream `#FDF6F4` (backdrop)
- Cocoa `#3D2A2A` (text + CTA)
- Rose `#C4677A` (accent)
- Plus textSecondary, stateGood, stateWarn (accessibility-darkened)

**Diagnosis:** the palette is **right** but missing **one luxury anchor** and the **WCAG-derived sage/amber feel utilitarian, not jeweled**.

**Recommendations:**

1. **Add a luxury anchor: warm ivory `#F4EADE`** — Hermès packaging, Gucci Décor, Loewe boutique base. Use this for secondary surfaces (Becoming chapter title backgrounds, modal sheets) so cream stays the hero backdrop and ivory becomes the "second cream." This is the Aesop trick — two warm whites instead of one + grey ([Brand trends 2026 — luxury minimalism](https://freethebirds.com/thoughts/brand-trends-2026-minimalism-vs-maximalism-the-aesthetics-of-feeling/)).

2. **Do NOT add chrome silver or iridescent as a system color.** Those read 2023 Y2K-revival, not 2026 luxury-warm. Keep iridescence to the bow sticker itself; never let it bleed into UI surfaces.

3. **Treat rose as TWO tones**, not one:
   - `roseHero #C4677A` for the cocoa-pill CTA hover/active and one celebration glow
   - `rosePetal #E8C2C9` for soft fills (like a Glossier Cloud Paint "Beam") on Becoming chart endpoints, sticker shadows, subtle highlights
   - This is the Mejuri/Glossier discipline — one saturated hero color + one whisper version, never the same color at two opacities.

4. **Replace utilitarian sage/amber with jeweled equivalents:**
   - `stateGood` from `#5F7345` → `#4D6A5A` (deeper, more Hermès-leather-green than sage utility)
   - `stateWarn` from `#8D6A2E` → `#9A6B2A` (slightly warmer, less office-folder amber)
   - Both keep WCAG AA contrast; both read "jewelry case" not "form validation".

5. **Add one "depth" tone:** `cocoaDeep #2A1A1A` for body text against ivory surfaces, so the hero cocoa CTA stays distinctly punchier than running text. Right now cocoa does double duty.

**Resulting locked palette (8 tokens, Cereal-style discipline):**

```
cream     #FDF6F4   primary backdrop
ivory     #F4EADE   secondary surface, modal, Becoming chapter base
cocoa     #3D2A2A   CTA + accent text
cocoaDeep #2A1A1A   body text (especially on ivory)
roseHero  #C4677A   one celebration accent
rosePetal #E8C2C9   chart endpoint, sticker shadow, soft fill
stateGood #4D6A5A   positive state (jeweled, not utility)
stateWarn #9A6B2A   warning state (jeweled)
```

## 7. The hero food card visual treatment

**Today:** cocoa-filled card with single ring + weekly avg bar.

**Diagnosis:** cocoa-fill is **almost right**. The issue is the weekly-avg bar competing with the ring inside the same card. Glossier/Mejuri product heroes always have **one focal element** + supporting text in caption position, never two equal data viz inside one container.

**Mejuri / Glossier pattern for product hero:**
- Single styled image fills 70% of card height
- Product name in lowercase serif at top-left, 22pt
- Price + one-line description in 12pt caption position at bottom
- No second graph, no chip row, no badges

**JeniFit equivalent:**

```
┌──────────────────────────────┐    ← cocoa fill #3D2A2A, 24pt corners
│  today's plate               │    ← Fraunces 22pt, cream text
│                              │
│         ◯                    │    ← single ring, 140pt, cream stroke
│      1,240 / 1,800           │    ← Fraunces numeric 28pt
│                              │
│  enough room for dinner ♥    │    ← Fraunces italic caption 14pt
│                          ❀   │    ← flower3D 24pt corner, food category
└──────────────────────────────┘

Weekly avg bar → moves OUT of hero card → lives in Becoming under "what you ate"
```

This is the Aesop product detail page move: **one fact per surface**. Weekly average is a Becoming concern, not a Home concern. Today's plate on Home, week-of-eating on Becoming. The narrative split mirrors the tab semantics already locked.

## 8. Becoming chapter visual rhythm — editorial publication mode

**Today:** 5 chapters stacked as document-style sections with italic-Fraunces titles + content blocks. Reads as "long dashboard."

**The Cereal / Aesop Library / Skims editorial move** is to give each chapter a **full-width opening spread** before content begins:

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                          │
│                                          │
│                ◠bow                      │  ← one sticker
│                                          │
│  I.                                      │  ← roman numeral 10pt eyebrow
│                                          │
│  your week                               │  ← 36pt lowercase Fraunces
│  ♥                                       │
│                                          │
│  a quiet read of how the last seven days │  ← 14pt body caption
│  felt in your body.                      │
│                                          │
│  — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — —   │
└──────────────────────────────────────────┘
                  [scroll]
┌──────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  (content blocks — bento data tiles)     │
└──────────────────────────────────────────┘
                  [scroll]
┌──────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  II.                                     │
│                                          │
│  what you ate                            │
│  ❀                                       │  ← flower sticker, food family
│                                          │
│  trend over data — the week not the day. │
│                                          │
│  — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — —   │
└──────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**The rhythm rules:**
- Each chapter open takes a **full viewport height** with maybe 15% content density — this is the Cereal Magazine move, and the Hermès "Venture Beyond" full-width illustration approach
- A **hairline rule** (0.5pt cocoa, 60% horizontal width, centered) closes each chapter opening before content begins — the editorial transition mark
- **Numbered roman numerals** (I, II, III, IV, V) sit above each title in 10pt all-caps DM Sans with +120ms letterspacing — Hermès / luxury editorial convention
- One **chapter-specific sticker** per opening (bow for week, flower for food, hearts for movement, sparkle for what's changing, cherries for what's worked) — semantic sticker placement = the Glossier Cloud Paint discipline applied to data
- A **one-sentence pull caption** under each chapter title in 14pt Fraunces italic — gives the chapter a tone-of-voice intro before data, the Aesop "The Fabulist" move

This converts Becoming from "long dashboard" to "browseable publication." It dramatically reduces perceived density (each chapter feels like a page-turn) without losing any data. The locked italic-Fraunces voice gets a job that's not "punch word in every sentence" — it becomes the chapter pull-caption, which is where it does its strongest work.

**Performance note:** the chapter-open full-viewport approach also creates **a natural lazy-load boundary** — the bento data tiles below the fold for chapters III-V don't need to render until scrolled. Editorial rhythm = perf win.

## Locked decisions (for the founder to merge into MEMORY)

1. **80/20 composition** — 80% restraint, 20% warmth, max ONE hot element per viewport
2. **Sticker = signature mark, not decoration** — 1 hero + 1 margin per surface, semantic family per surface
3. **Two warm whites** — cream `#FDF6F4` (Home backdrop) + ivory `#F4EADE` (Becoming + modal base)
4. **Numbers in Fraunces tabular**, not DM Sans — Tiffany/Mejuri jeweled-numeric move
5. **Becoming = editorial publication** — full-viewport chapter opens with roman numeral + sticker + pull-caption, hairline transitions
6. **Weekly avg bar moves OFF Home food card** — one fact per surface; weekly lives in Becoming "what you ate"
7. **Hairlines, not borders, between data blocks on Home** — scrapbook chrome reserved for the 1-2 hero cards
8. **Lowercase headlines all the way down to eyebrows** — including roman numerals (`I.`, not `I.`-stylized)

## Open questions for design QA

- Should the cocoa "today, jen ♥" note retain its bordered scrapbook chrome, or strip to flat-on-cream so the food hero becomes the only chrome'd element above fold? (Lean: strip the note border, give chrome only to the food hero — pushes more luxury-restraint.)
- Should chapter-open stickers be larger than current peripheral stickers (48pt vs 32pt) to act as chapter-marker hero? Test on device — the spec above suggests yes.
- Does the workout card stay on Home or migrate to a fourth tab? Current "demoted chrome" is a tell that the position isn't earning. Worth a separate spec.
