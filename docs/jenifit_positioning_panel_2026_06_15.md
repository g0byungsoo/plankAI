## 1. Executive Summary

The panel converges on a positioning the current product is already 80% built for, that the founder is currently 0% marketing: **the 84-day program for women who can't stop thinking about food.** Five experts, scoring 5 options across 30+ dimensions, arrive at the same call from different doors.

Aggregate: **E (270) > C (264) > B (209) > A (187) > D (174).** E is the most-named #1; C is second-most. The Growth marketer's B-first call is rejected by the VC (telehealth absorbs it) and the Operator (compliance kills a solo founder). The PMF researcher resolves the C-vs-E tension cleanly: **C is the cohort. E is the wrapper. They are complementary, not competing.**

**The strategic call in one sentence:** JeniFit is the food-noise-recovery app (E-frame) for women who tried everything — with the post-GLP-1 maintenance cohort (C-cohort) as the wedge into the broader "tried everything" market, monetized through an 84-day CBT curriculum (the existing 75%-completion asset), packaged in a voice the cohort already speaks (post-Ozempic, lowercase, no shame, no streaks).

**The five non-negotiables of v2 (revised):**

1. **Drop "weight loss" from the App Store subtitle and paywall headline.** Four of five experts independently demanded this.
2. **The 84-day CBT curriculum becomes the named product**, not "the app's curriculum." Branded, completable, peer-reviewed-adjacent. 75% completion is your only PMF signal — bet on it.
3. **The calorie scanner becomes the marketing hero, the curriculum becomes the product hero.** Scanner = TikTok-friendly 7-second demo, lets you steal Cal AI's traffic. Curriculum = retention engine that survives Apple shipping a scanner in iOS 27.
4. **Pause TikTok account expansion.** The Operator's math is dispositive: 200-300 views × 3-6% paywall = ~10 trial intents/post = no learning signal. **Fix the paywall before generating one more post.**
5. **Cap accounts at 4-5 — never 50-100.** Both the Operator and Growth expert agree the marginal return turns negative past 12 accounts; the Operator says solo-founder ceiling is 5-8 with cognitive load; below that you can still respond to comments, which is the actual retention loop.

**The single highest-leverage 14-day ship (May 15-29):** paywall A/B with new CBT/food-noise positioning + App Store CPP per content lane + share-card export from the scanner. Expected lift: paywall→trial from 3-6% to 8-12%, halving blended CAC without a single new post. Total dev time: ~30 hours.

## 2. Market Analysis

### Hard sizing (mid-2026, women 22-35)

| Segment | US size | Global | Growth | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Currently on a GLP-1 | ~5M | 12-15M | 35% YoY | RAND 2025-08, IQVIA |
| Just stopped a GLP-1 (last 6 mo) | ~1.5-2M | ~5M | 40-60% YoY | Lancet eClinMed regain meta |
| PCOS / insulin resistance | ~5M | ~30M | 30-50% YoY | Coherent Mkt Insights |
| "Tried everything," app-fatigued | ~12M | ~60M | flat | NHANES |
| Actively trying to lose 10-20 lb without drugs | ~22M | ~120M | -5% YoY | KFF 2025 |

**The post-GLP-1 cohort is the fastest-growing segment in women's weight loss, will be the larger cohort by 2027, and is owned by nobody.** That last fact is the entire investment thesis.

### The 5 shifts changing what apps need to do (next 3-5 years)

1. **Tracking becomes a feature, not a product.** iOS 27 ships a scanner. MyFitnessPal already free-tier-ifies macros. By 2027 every photo app does it.
2. **CBT/behavioral curricula become the moat.** Noom proved it (43.6% D30). Curriculum IP is harder to clone than a vision model.
3. **Algorithmic vocabulary tightens.** SkinnyTok-banned vocab keeps expanding. The apps that win marketing are the ones that *don't say "weight loss"* in their hook.
4. **Pharma normalizes the off-ramp.** Lilly/Novo fund maintenance protocols because regain hurts their brand. Apps that own the *exit* get partnership distribution.
5. **Body composition replaces weight as the daily metric.** Withings/Oura/Eight Sleep push muscle mass and visceral fat into the daily ring. Weight tracking becomes a back-of-app utility.

### The competitive landscape, refreshed

- **Cal AI delisted April 2026** (deceptive billing) → "ai calorie counter" keyword + 12K US monthly searches freed up. MFP+Cal AI consolidated under Francisco Partners → integrating, not innovating, for 12-18 months.
- **Noom** retains 43.6% D30 via human coaches. Unmatched on retention, but Noom Med's prescriber business creates a structural conflict of interest when telling patients to come off GLP-1s. **JeniFit's non-prescribing status is its moat against Noom on the post-GLP-1 cohort.**
- **BetterMe** dominant on paid acquisition. Outspending them is not viable; outpositioning them is.
- **Calibrate** wounded post-layoffs, structurally a sitting duck for the C-cohort pivot — assume they'll attempt it within 12 months.
- **MeAgain (~372K users), Shotsy (~250K)** — GLP-1 *active companion* category is filling fast. Don't enter; outflank.

## 4. Recommended Positioning

### The brand line

**"the 84-day program for women who can't stop thinking about food."**

Drop "weight loss" from the App Store subtitle. Drop "calorie counter" from the icon-name. Lead with **food noise** as the named problem and **the 84-day program** as the named solution.

### The wedge

**The post-GLP-1 maintenance cohort** (women 26-34 who came off Ozempic/Wegovy/Mounjaro/Zepbound in the last 6-18 months and have already regained 8-15 lb or are terrified they will). This is your beachhead. Onboarding has a fork on day 1: "have you taken a GLP-1?" → Yes branch routes to post-GLP-1 protocol with muscle-preservation + food-noise-rebuild lessons. No branch routes to the generic 84-day curriculum.

### The brand voice (refined)

Current voice (lowercase, Fraunces italic punch words, post-Ozempic vocab) is **80% right.** The 20% to fix per the Psychology expert: too "clean-girl therapy-speak." Add dry self-aware wit. Sound like **Olipop, not Glossier.** Examples:

- ❌ "Today, gently rebuild trust with your body."
- ✓ "we're not gonna make you log a cucumber."
- ✓ "the scale lied today. it does that."
- ✓ "monday was loud. thursday you didn't think about food until 2pm. that's the thing we're tracking."

Complicity > care. The cohort responds to **friendship that gets it**, not to therapy-language.

### What gets killed

- ❌ "AI" anywhere in the App Store listing, marketing, or in-app copy (already done — keep it that way)
- ❌ "Weight loss" in the App Store subtitle, headline, or paywall
- ❌ "Streaks" — already absent; do not let any future PM reintroduce them
- ❌ "Before/after" content in marketing — SkinnyTok-flagged
- ❌ The workout engine as a primary value prop on the App Store (push to screenshot 4 or 5)
- ❌ Any "GLP-1 companion" framing that triggers FDA-adjacent compliance overhead
- ❌ Any "coach" or "chatbot" framing

### What gets added

- ✓ "Food noise" named explicitly on App Store screenshot 1
- ✓ "84-day program" as the unique-claim noun (every reference to the curriculum names the day count)
- ✓ Post-GLP-1 fork in onboarding (Q1: "have you tried a GLP-1?")
- ✓ Muscle-preservation protein/resistance protocol surfaced for the C-cohort
- ✓ Share-card export from the scanner (the Cal-AI-style screenshot moment)
- ✓ Weekly "Sunday read" narrative artifact per Psychology expert's retention loop design
- ✓ "Noise score" as the daily metric (replaces calorie count as the salient number)

**Days 61-90:** Layer first paid creator partnership ($200-400/clip × 3) and Meta Spark on top-3 organic winners.

### Content pillars (the 5 that survived the panel)

1. **The scanner reveal** — 7-second screen recording of the JeniFit scanner on a "wellness" food. Best demo any food app has in 2026. Hook formula: **[wellness item] + [shocking macro reveal]**. Format: reaction + screen-record. Pillar archetype: "you scanned your erewhon smoothie."
2. **The food noise diary** — Sunday-read excerpts as TikTok content. Hook formula: **[day name] + [contrarian observation about her own brain]**. Format: text overlay + voice-over. Pillar archetype: "monday was loud. thursday you didn't think about food until 2pm."
3. **Post-GLP-1 panic** — content for the C-cohort. Hook formula: **[day off drug] + [specific fear or system]**. Format: talking-head (UGC creator) + carousel. Pillar archetype: "day 1 off mounjaro. here's the plan."
4. **The 84-day reveal** — lesson-by-lesson teaser. Hook formula: **[day N of 84] + [counter-intuitive lesson]**. Format: text-on-photo carousel. Pillar archetype: "day 12: why you binge after a fight."
5. **The "tried everything" confession** — pure POV. Hook formula: **[failed prior attempt] + [why it failed mechanistically]**. Format: text-only photo. Pillar archetype: "myfitnesspal made me worse. here's why."

4. **Share-card exporter (in-app)** — PIL or iOS UIKit composite of food photo + scanner card + "scanned with jenefit" watermark. **~6 hrs iOS.**

### What NOT to build

1. ❌ TikTok upload API integration — irrelevant under 10K followers per prior research; reapply 2027.
2. ❌ 50-account orchestration with proxies/VPS — solo founder cannot operate this. Cap at 5 accounts manually.
3. ❌ AI reaction-face generation — flagged as synthetic by TikTok 2026. Stick with paid Billo creators.
4. ❌ MakeUGC-style "AI authority figure" — e-com-coded, brand inconsistent with JeniFit's anti-AI voice.

### The content workflow

```
Weekly cadence (solo founder, ~6 hrs/week total):
- Monday 9am  : review last week's view counts; rank top 3 hooks
- Monday 10am : Claude generates 15 new hook variants in the style of the top 3
- Tuesday     : edit + queue 7 carousels in Postiz (1/day × 5 accounts + 2 buffer)
```

No automated trend-scraping tools (TikTok rate-limits aggressively; they're brittle). Manual screenshot review is faster and cheaper.

### Feedback loops

## 8. Risks & Failure Modes

### The strongest argument against this strategy

**The C-cohort is too small in 2026.** Post-GLP-1 women who've come off the drug in the last 6 months are ~1.5-2M in the US. If JeniFit converts 0.5% of them in year 1, that's 10K paying users × $60 ARR = $600K ARR. Below Series Seed threshold and below the operator's $80K MRR fund-ability bar. **What if the cohort isn't ready yet and we're 12 months early?**

**The honest answer:** That's why E (food noise) is the wrapper, not C. The food-noise frame catches the GLP-1-curious-but-not-on cohort, the just-off cohort, the never-on cohort, AND the cohort 12 months from coming off — without the timing risk of pure-C positioning. E-with-C-onboarding-fork is the hedge.

### The biggest risks ranked

1. **Apple ships a CBT-coded weight management module in iOS 27 Health.** Probability: ~30%. Mitigation: brand voice + curriculum specificity. Apple doesn't ship opinionated lowercase-Fraunces content; they ship infrastructure. JeniFit owns the *voice*, not the *feature*.
2. **Noom Med pivots to post-GLP-1 maintenance positioning.** Probability: ~60% in 24 months. Mitigation: **be there first.** This is why the 9-15 month window matters. Plant the flag before Noom Med's brand can credibly own "off-ramp."
3. **The 75% lesson completion is a self-selection artifact.** Probability: ~20%. Mitigation: 21-day MVT (see Operator's section 6 spec). If CBT-positioned cohort retention plateaus below 25% D30, the curriculum's stickiness was an early-adopter illusion and the bet is broken.
4. **Paywall fix doesn't move conversion.** Probability: ~25%. Mitigation: if paywall stays at 6% after 3 redesigns, the problem is pricing or product-fit, not copy. Pivot pricing to a $4.99/mo entry-tier with curriculum-only access.
5. **TikTok algorithm tightens further on weight-loss-adjacent content.** Probability: ~50%. Mitigation: food-noise vocabulary is currently NOT on the suppressed list. But assume it could be in 12 months; have a backup brand voice using "interoception," "appetite regulation," "satiety," "mindful," ready to swap.
6. **Lilly/Novo enter the maintenance app market directly.** Probability: ~20%. Mitigation: partner before pivot. Reach out to a clinical contact at Novo Nordisk's Saxenda team in Q3.
7. **Solo founder burnout from running 5 accounts + product + comments + email.** Probability: ~40%. Mitigation: hard cap at 5 accounts. Contract a $1.5K/mo CBT-credentialed reviewer for content brief approval. Don't scale until D90 retention proves you can sell to acquisition.

### Early warning indicators (review weekly)

| Indicator | Healthy | Warning | Kill |
|---|---|---|---|
| Paywall → trial | ≥8% | 5-8% | <5% after 2 redesigns |
| D7 retention | ≥45% | 35-45% | <30% |
| D30 retention | ≥32% | 22-32% | <20% |
| Lesson 1→3 completion | ≥75% | 60-75% | <60% (curriculum stickiness lost) |
| Best TikTok post (rolling 30d) | >25K views | 10-25K | <10K = format dead |
| Comment sentiment | "this is me" type | engagement-bait type | hostility or shame triggers |
| Weekly cost (paid + tools) | <$300 | $300-500 | >$500 with CAC > $35 |

### What would cause this strategy to fail

- The 75% lesson completion was a self-selection artifact; the curriculum doesn't generalize to TikTok-acquired users → **kills E-frame**.
- Telehealth providers refuse to refer off-ramp users to a non-clinical app → **kills C-distribution**.
- Solo founder cannot fix the paywall in 14 days while running content → **kills sequencing**.
- The "food noise" vocabulary gets co-opted by a competitor (Noom Med, WW) within 6 months → **forces brand pivot to a sharper, less-common term**.

## Appendix: What was rejected and why

- **GLP-1 active companion app (B):** 4 of 5 experts said no. Compliance overhead kills solo founder; telehealth players (Hims, Ro, Noom Med) will own this within 24 months.

- **AI chatbot coach:** 5 of 5 experts said no. Failed publicly elsewhere (Lark, Vida, Woebot).
- **Generic "AI" branding anywhere:** Already absent. Keep it that way per [[feedback-skinnytok-ban-vocab]].
