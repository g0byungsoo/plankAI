# Art-direction composition rubric — JeniFit hybrid-editorial heroes (2026-06-10)

Written from an editorial art-director lens (Cereal / Kinfolk / Cherry
Bombe / Apartamento). Scope: composition rules for the **Direction A
hybrid-editorial** hero beats — real-photo collages on cream `#FDF6F4`,
cocoa `#3D2A2A` typography, and coquette 3D sticker accents.

The founder rejected AI-generated 3D-illustration heroes for three
reasons: (1) backgrounds clashed with cream, (2) cartoonish register,
(3) no editorial weight. This document fixes all three by treating
photos as **load-bearing composition**, not decoration — the same move
her75 makes with its lifestyle photography.

> Constraint to hold throughout: **cream is the sheet of paper, photos
> are stamps adhered onto it, stickers are stickers on top of stamps.**
> If a photo brings its own background, it is no longer a stamp — it is
> a tile, and the page becomes a Pinterest grid. The whole rubric is
> downstream of this single rule.

---

## 1. The collage grammar her75 uses

her75's composition isn't a "mood board." It's a 3-tier hierarchy with
strict edge behavior, applied identically across IMG_6275, IMG_6278, and
IMG_6282. Naming the rules makes them reusable.

### 1.1 Three-tier size hierarchy

her75 never ships more than 3 size registers per hero:

- **Anchor** — one element, ~38–46% of viewport width or a centered
  220–280pt round portrait. The reader's eye lands here first.
  *Examples:* the round avatar in IMG_6275 (centered, ~210pt circle);
  the full-body hallway photo in IMG_6278 (~320pt tall, near-full
  width); the "+200,000 joined" hugging trio inside the cocoa pill in
  IMG_6282 (~210pt wide capsule).
- **Flankers** — 2 to 4 mid-tier photos at ~22–28% of viewport width,
  hugging corners, *bleeding 30–40% off the edge*. They frame the
  anchor without competing.
  *Examples:* IMG_6275 ocean foam top-left, book stack top-right, lily
  bouquet bottom-left, matcha bottom-right — all 4 bleed off the safe
  area.
- **Accents** — 0 to 2 stickers / pills / tiny artifacts at ≤12% of
  viewport width. The pill social-proof slot ("Among 24,000+ women")
  lives in this register, as does any glossy sticker punctuation.

What her75 **never** does: a uniform-grid layout. Tier mixing is the
whole point — without the size collapse there's no hierarchy, and
without hierarchy the page reads as Pinterest.

### 1.2 Edge-bleed is mandatory for flankers

IMG_6275's four corner photos all bleed past the safe area. None of
them are contained. This is the load-bearing move: bleed says "the
page is a fragment of a bigger world," contained says "these are tiles
in a grid." The eye reads bleed as editorial, contain as catalogue.

**Rule:** every flanker bleeds at least 25% of its own width off the
nearest edge. If you cannot bleed it (e.g. it carries critical content),
demote it to an accent.

### 1.3 The center column stays clear

In IMG_6275, IMG_6277, and IMG_6282, the **vertical center 50%** of the
viewport carries headline + pill + CTA *only*. Photos live left and
right of this column, or above and below it. IMG_6278 is the exception
— a single anchor photo *is* the hero, and the headline drops below it.

**Rule:** if a photo encroaches on the center 50%, it becomes the
anchor and there can be no other anchor on the page.

### 1.4 Tilt + overlap behavior

her75 mostly ships photos **axis-aligned** (0° tilt). The only tilted
element is the IMG_6278 "day one" paper sticker (~3° tilt, applied as
if hand-stuck). Adjacent photos overlap **6–12pt** at corners — enough
to read as "stacked," not enough to obscure subject matter.

**Rule:** photos at 0°. Stickers at ±5–8°. Never both tilted on the
same surface — the page starts to spin.

### 1.5 The pill is the bridge

The cocoa social-proof pill ("Among 24,000+ women", "+30% success with
friends", "+200,000 joined") is doing a specific composition job: it
**closes the visual gap between photo cluster and headline**. Without
it, the headline floats and the photo cluster reads as unrelated to
the words. With it, the page resolves as one editorial unit.

**Rule:** any hero with a photo cluster + headline ships a pill
between them. The pill is grounded in a real number (data-provenance
rule). One pill maximum per hero.

### 1.6 Sticker-to-photo relationship in her75

her75 uses **one** sticker-on-photo composite (IMG_6278's "day one"
paper-card adhered to the woman in the hallway). That single move
carries the whole "Make it official" beat. Elsewhere, there are zero
stickers — the photos themselves carry the visual weight.

**Translation rule for JeniFit (where stickers are part of the brand
pack):** sticker scatter exists, but it is **reduced to ≤2 stickers**
on any hero that ships a photo cluster. The photo cluster *replaces*
~80% of the sticker scatter's compositional job. Stickers become
punctuation, not structure.

## 2. The 5-photo collage formula for JeniFit

A reusable formula JeniFit can apply across every hero moment that
calls for editorial weight. Tuned for iPhone aspect (~9:18) and the
cream `#FDF6F4` constraint.

```
Anchor      = 1 photo, 200–240pt round OR 280–340pt soft-rect
Flankers    = 2 photos, 110–140pt, edge-bleeding, opposite corners
Accents     = 2 stickers, 36–48pt, ±6° tilt, opposite corners from flankers
Bridge      = 1 social-proof pill, 13pt cocoa-fill DM SemiBold
Headline    = Fraunces SemiBold 42pt, 1 italic punch word
```

### 2.1 Anchor placement

- **Round-portrait anchor** (IMG_6275 mode): centered horizontally,
  vertical top edge at ~22% from screen top. Subject framed neck-up OR
  hand-holding-object (camera, mug). Inside a 1.5pt cocoa stroke ring
  at 8% opacity — almost invisible, just enough to separate from cream.
- **Soft-rect anchor** (IMG_6278 mode): 20pt corner radius, centered or
  slight-left offset, vertical top at ~12%. Bleeds past viewport
  horizontally if subject permits.

### 2.2 Flanker placement

Two flankers, always on **opposite corners** (top-left + bottom-right,
or top-right + bottom-left). Never adjacent corners — that crowds one
half of the page. Each bleeds 25–40% of its own width off the screen
edge.

### 2.3 Accent placement

Two stickers from `Assets.xcassets/Stickers/`, placed on the **two
remaining corners** (the ones the flankers didn't claim). Size 36–48pt
(smaller than the 56–72pt the her75 extraction doc suggests for
photo-less heroes — when photos are present, stickers must shrink to
stay accent-tier). Tilt ±6°. Painterly-style stickers preferred (gummy
bear, iridescent bow, perfume bottle, disco ball) — line-art stickers
get visually consumed by the photo competition.

### 2.4 Color cap — max 3 distinct hues across the photo set

The single highest-leverage rule. Across all 3 photos (1 anchor + 2
flankers), the **combined palette must collapse to ≤3 dominant hues**.
Otherwise the page reads as Pinterest.

The 3 allowed hue families for JeniFit:
1. **Warm cream / blush / dusty-rose** (photos of bouquets, satin
   ribbons, cream linens, peach skin tones, latte foam)
2. **Cocoa / mocha / sienna** (photos of espresso, leather, dark
   wood grain, espresso-stained sugar, lipstick)
3. **One supporting hue** picked per surface — sage green (matcha,
   foliage), buttery yellow (honey, lemon, books with cream pages),
   or pearl-white (pearls, white linen). **One** supporting hue per
   surface, not all three.

What this excludes on sight: cyan/teal pools, electric blue gym
photos, neon orange citrus, lime green smoothie, true black phone
screens, fluorescent gym lighting, magenta floral arrangements.

### 2.5 Saturation cap — desaturate every photo to ~85%

Even when hues are correct, full-saturation photos on cream look like
cutouts from a magazine ad. her75 ships photos at perceptible
desaturation (~85% of native saturation in IMG_6275's bouquet and
matcha). The eye reads desaturated photos as *belonging to the page*,
not pasted on.

**Process rule:** in Lightroom / Photoshop, drop Saturation -15 and
Vibrance -10 on every photo before delivery. The cream pickup is the
result — photos start to share air with the bgPrimary.

### 2.6 The 5-element budget is a ceiling, not a floor

A 5-photo collage is the **maximum** for any JeniFit hero. Most heroes
ship at 3 elements (1 anchor + 2 flankers, no stickers). The IMG_6280
"Personalizing your space" beat ships **zero** elements — silent
typography is its own register and must be preserved as the contrast.

## 3. How photos sit on cream `#FDF6F4`

Cream is not white. It carries a measurable warm-magenta cast (R 253,
G 246, B 244 → +9 magenta vs neutral grey at the same luminance).
Photos with cool/blue casts will fight it visibly. Photos with their
own warm-rose cast will harmonize.

### 3.1 Cutout vs preserved-background — decide per photo

- **Cutout** (background removed in Photoshop, subject placed on
  cream): use for photos whose subject is the entire point and whose
  original background would clash. Bouquets, single objects, food,
  hands holding objects, single-figure portraits where the room is
  irrelevant. Cutout edges must be feathered 1.5–2px — hard cutouts
  look like clip-art.
- **Preserved background**: use only when the photo's original
  background is *already in the JeniFit palette* — warm-cream
  interiors, soft-rose fabric, mocha-tile coffee shops. her75
  IMG_6278's hallway photo preserves its grey-mint wall because the
  wall is desaturated enough to read as paper texture.

**Default rule:** when in doubt, cut out. ~80% of JeniFit heroes
should ship cutout photos on cream. Preserved-background photos are
the exception, used 1× per cluster maximum to avoid the "everything
looks like a sticker" effect.

### 3.2 Warm-white edge halo

When cutting out photos, render a **2–3px halo at #FFFAF8** (the
`bgElevated` token) around the subject. Pure-cream halo blends into
the page; slightly-brighter halo provides a hair of separation. This
is what makes a cutout read as a *paper stamp* instead of a floating
PNG. Most apps skip this. Don't.

### 3.3 Anti-magenta-cast rule

Reject any photo whose dominant background or subject has a magenta /
purple / cool-pink cast. The cream is already +9 magenta — a magenta
subject doubles the cast and the whole page goes saccharine.

Allowed pinks: dusty rose, blush, peach, salmon, terracotta.
Rejected pinks: hot pink, fuchsia, magenta, lavender, lilac, neon
coral.

### 3.4 Black point limit — no #000 in any photo

Even desaturated, a photo with a true black region (a phone screen, a
shadow, a leather jacket) punches a hole in the page. Cocoa
`#3D2A2A` is the deepest tone JeniFit ever ships. Photos must
clamp their shadows to roughly the same depth — in Lightroom, lift
Blacks +15 to +25 on delivery. The page will *feel* held together
even if the viewer can't articulate why.

### 3.5 Grain texture — 2–4% film grain

her75 ships subtle film grain across all its photography (IMG_6275 is
the clearest case — the bouquet and ocean foam both carry visible
grain). Grain serves two purposes: it unifies photos shot in
different conditions, and it bridges the texture gap between photo
and cream paper.

**Process rule:** 2–4% monochrome grain overlay on every delivered
photo. Below 2% disappears; above 4% reads as Instagram filter.

## 4. Hero-photo crop rules

### 4.1 Always crop OUT

- **Faces above the eyes.** Her75 crops at hairline-or-below or shows
  hand-over-face (IMG_6275 mirror selfie). Eyes-only crops are
  intimate and feel like a real person, not a stock model. Full faces
  read as ads. This rule keeps JeniFit out of "weight-loss before/after
  marketing" register, which the founder has explicitly forbidden.
- **Brand logos.** Coffee cup logos, book titles with brand marks,
  phone-back Apple logos. They date the photo and break the editorial
  voice. Photoshop out before delivery.
- **Time-of-day giveaways.** Mid-day harsh sun shadows (read as
  outdoor / athletic) — keep golden-hour, morning-window, or
  diffused-indoor lighting only. Hard shadows fight the warm-cream
  softness.
- **Tech objects.** Smart watches, ring lights, RGB peripherals,
  obvious AirPods. Read as gadget-app, not editorial.
- **Logos / typography on objects.** Book covers with bold titles,
  shampoo bottles, branded coffee sleeves.
- **Other people in the background.** The page is intimate; a third
  figure breaks the intimacy.

### 4.2 Always crop IN

- **Hands** — hands holding a mug, hands typing, hands flipping a
  journal page. Hands carry agency without identity (the model could
  be any reader). her75 IMG_6275's hand-holding-camera is the textbook
  case.
- **Objects with daily-ritual subtext** — espresso cups, journals,
  matcha glasses, fresh-cut flowers, linen, candle wicks just lit. The
  Cereal magazine subject set, exactly.
- **Skin tones** — when faces appear, keep skin to neck-down or
  partial (the IMG_6275 selfie). Show a forearm, a collarbone, a
  hairline. Inclusion without identity.
- **Negative space within the photo itself.** Photos with their own
  internal whitespace (e.g. a single matcha glass on a wide table)
  give the eye room and read as composed, not crowded.

### 4.3 Aspect-ratio rules

- **Anchor photos**: 1:1 (round-portrait) or 4:5 (soft-rect tall).
  Never 16:9 — landscape ratios read as desktop-software.
- **Flanker photos**: 1:1 preferred. 3:4 acceptable if the subject
  demands height (a tall bouquet, a stack of books).
- **Edge-bleeding flankers**: the bleed direction matters — bleed the
  *less interesting* edge of the photo (the side without subject), so
  the subject stays in the visible half.

## 5. Sticker-as-accent composition

The coquette sticker pack is part of JeniFit's brand. With photos
present, stickers downshift from structure to punctuation. Three
relationships govern where stickers land:

### 5.1 Stickers ON photos (the "adhered paper" move)

- **Use 1× per hero maximum.** IMG_6278 is the model — one paper
  artifact stuck on the main photo carries the whole beat.
- **Sticker scale: 30–40% of the photo's smaller dimension.**
  Big enough to read, small enough not to obscure the subject.
- **Tilt: ±3–6°.** Hand-stuck register.
- **Placement: subject-adjacent, never subject-covering.** Stick the
  sticker over the shoulder, the corner of the journal, the lower-
  third of the photo — never the face.
- **Shadow under the sticker:** subtle warm-rose drop (the
  `.plankShadow()` modifier already exists). Sells the "physical
  paper" register.
- **Best sticker types for on-photo:** painterly satin (bow_satin,
  flower_3d, gummy_bear). Line-art stickers disappear against
  photographic detail — keep them off photos.

### 5.2 Stickers NEAR photos (corner-accent register)

- **Use 0–2 per hero.** Placed in the corners the flankers didn't
  claim.
- **Sticker scale: 36–48pt** when photos are present. (Photo-less
  heroes can use the 56–72pt "structural" sticker per the
  her75-extraction doc; photo heroes must shrink.)
- **Tilt: ±6–8°.**
- **Hug the corner — center the sticker on the corner point.** The
  sticker reads as "placed on the edge of the page."
- **Allowed stickers near photos:** anything from the painterly pack.
  Iridescent stickers (disco_ball, candy_iridescent, butterfly_ring)
  add a coquette finish without competing on hue.

### 5.3 Stickers SEPARATE from photos (rare)

- **Use only when the photo cluster carries 0 stickers AND a single
  sticker is needed as a typographic ornament.** E.g. an italic-
  Fraunces title with a single iridescent bow sticker hung off the
  italic punch word.
- **Sticker scale: 24–32pt** in this register (it functions like a
  glyph, not a graphic).
- **Tilt: 0°** when used as a typographic ornament.
- **Placement: anchored to the italic word's baseline or descender.**

### 5.4 Hard rules

- **Never put a sticker behind a CTA.** Per `THEME.md` §4.
- **Never put a sticker on top of body copy.** Hero headline only,
  and only in the typographic-ornament register.
- **Never mix line-art and painterly stickers in the same on-photo
  composition.** Pick one family per surface; visual register collapses
  otherwise.

## 7. Photo Sourcing Brief (1-page template)

Hand this to a freelance art director, or paste into Midjourney /
Flux for AI-curated photography. Edit the *Surface* and *Beat* fields
per assignment.

```
JENIFIT PHOTO SOURCING BRIEF
============================

Surface:        [e.g. pre-paywall reveal]
Beat:           [e.g. "your plan is ready" — quiet competence,
                pre-commitment, deserving]
Quantity:       [e.g. 1 anchor + 2 flankers = 3 photos]
Delivery:       PSD or PNG with transparency, 3× iPhone (~1170pt wide),
                pre-graded per palette below

SUBJECT PALETTE (acceptable)
----------------------------
- Hands cradling objects (mugs, journals, books, flowers)
- Cut bouquets — peonies, tulips, lilies, ranunculus, sweet pea
- Espresso / matcha / cortado in clear glass or cream ceramic
- Open notebooks with handwritten pages (text illegible)
- Linen, cream cotton, raw silk fabrics
- Sliced fruit on small plates — peach, grapefruit, fig, persimmon
- Soft morning light through a window
- Lit candles, low flame
- Cream-pages books stacked (no titles visible)
- Shoulders, collarbones, forearms, hairlines (never full face)

SUBJECT PALETTE (reject)
------------------------
- Full faces, before/after body comparisons, gym mirror selfies
- Smart watches, AirPods, phone screens with UI visible
- Brand logos of any kind (coffee cups, books, clothing tags)
- Athletic wear, weights, protein shakes, gym equipment
- Children, multiple people in background, men
- Hot pink / fuchsia / magenta / lavender / neon coral subjects
- True black objects (leather jackets, phone backs, dark screens)
- Outdoor harsh-noon lighting, club lighting, RGB lighting
- Food in plastic containers, packaged products, fast food

LIGHTING PALETTE
----------------
- Soft morning window light, key from upper-left
- Diffused indoor overcast
- Golden-hour interior bounce
- Single-candle warm
NEVER: direct flash, ring light, fluorescent overhead, mid-day sun

COMPOSITION PALETTE
-------------------
- Square 1:1 or tall 4:5 framing only — never 16:9
- Subject occupies 40–70% of frame
- 30–60% internal negative space (the photo itself must breathe)
- Off-center subject placement preferred (rule of thirds)
- Single subject — never overlapping multiple competing objects
- Plain or texturally consistent background (linen, cream wall,
  pale wood, cream linen tablecloth)

COLOR PALETTE — POST-PRODUCTION TARGETS
---------------------------------------
- White balance: 4800K–5500K (warm side of neutral)
- Saturation: -15
- Vibrance: -10
- Blacks: lifted +15 to +25 (no #000 in delivered file)
- Highlights: -10 (preserve cream-zone detail)
- Grain: 2–4% monochrome film grain overlay
- Halo: 2–3px #FFFAF8 edge halo around cutout subjects
- Dominant hues per photo set must collapse to ≤3 hue families:
   (1) warm cream / blush / dusty rose
   (2) cocoa / mocha / sienna
   (3) ONE supporting hue per surface — sage, butter, or pearl-white

ITEMS TO INCLUDE
----------------
- 1 anchor (200–240pt round OR 280–340pt soft-rect)
- 2 flankers (110–140pt, edge-bleeding, opposite corners)
- All photos delivered with backgrounds REMOVED unless preserved
  background already matches the palette above

ITEMS TO REJECT ON SIGHT
------------------------
- Any photo that would look at home on a typical fitness-app store
  listing
- Any photo that reads as "stock" rather than "editorial"
- Any photo carrying a third hue family not in the surface's palette
- Any photo whose desaturated state still looks crowded or busy
```
